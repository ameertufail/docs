## Persona A: Solo Builder

<Card title="Solo Builder" icon="user">
  Building an AI feature in their app. Wants a minimal system to keep prompt
  versions stable and ship changes with confidence.
</Card>

**Background:** A solo developer or indie hacker building an AI-powered product. They're shipping fast and don't have time to build eval infrastructure from scratch.

**Pain points:**
- Accidentally breaks something with a small prompt change and has no way to know
- Manually tests prompts by pasting into ChatGPT — not repeatable
- No diff view to understand what actually changed between versions

**Key need:** _"Did my change make things worse?"_

**How PromptOps Studio helps:** A lightweight prompt versioning system + quick eval runs against a small golden set. Fast to set up, easy to maintain.

## Persona B: Startup AI/Full-Stack Engineer

<Card title="Startup Engineer" icon="users">
  On a small team shipping AI features fast. Needs datasets, automated eval
  runs, guardrails, and regression tracking.
</Card>

**Background:** An engineer at a 5–20 person startup where AI features are core to the product. The team ships weekly and prompt quality directly affects retention.

**Pain points:**
- Prompt changes by one engineer silently break something for another
- No systematic eval process — "trust me, I tested it" is the workflow
- JSON outputs keep failing schema validation in production
- No audit trail for who changed what and when

**Key need:** _"Can I safely promote this prompt to production?"_

**How PromptOps Studio helps:** Full eval workflow — datasets, automated runs, guardrails, release gates. A "deploy" workflow for prompts analogous to CI/CD for code.

## Persona C: Tech Lead / Product Owner

<Card title="Tech Lead / Product Owner" icon="chart-bar">
  Doesn't write prompts but approves changes. Wants dashboards and a clear
  report to approve or reject prompt changes.
</Card>

**Background:** A tech lead or product owner who signs off on AI feature changes. Technically literate but not writing prompts daily.

**Pain points:**
- Has to trust engineers' verbal summaries of prompt changes
- No quantitative signal for "is v2 better than v1?"
- Latency and cost increases are invisible until they hit the bill

**Key need:** _"Show me the data — is v2 better than v1?"_

**How PromptOps Studio helps:** Eval reports with clear pass/fail verdicts, regression highlights, judge score comparisons, and dashboard trends over time.

## Non-Targets (for MVP 0–2)

These are real use cases but intentionally out of scope until after MVP 2:

- Enterprise compliance/SSO/SAML users
- Teams that need fine-tuning or model training management
- Organizations requiring on-prem deployment with no cloud dependencies
- Users who need autonomous agent-builder workflows
