Building in public means sharing progress, learnings, and milestones openly as you build. It creates accountability, attracts early users, and turns the build itself into marketing.

## What to Post at Each Iteration

Every iteration has a ship checkpoint with a tweet template. The format is consistent:

1. **Screenshot or GIF** of the feature working — show, don't just tell
2. **Technical learning** — one thing you learned building it
3. **Next up** — builds anticipation

Use hashtags: `#BuildInPublic` `#LLMOps` `#OpenSource`

Post on: Twitter/X, LinkedIn, relevant Discord servers, Reddit (r/LocalLLaMA, r/MachineLearning)

## Content Ideas Between Milestones

Not just shipping posts — explain what you're building and why:

- **Architecture decisions:** "Why we chose browser-orchestrated eval execution over server-side" 
- **Technical deep-dives:** "How we store LLM provider keys securely with AES-256-GCM on Cloudflare D1"
- **Problem framing:** "The four LLMOps problems we kept hitting on every AI project"
- **Comparison posts:** "PromptOps Studio vs Braintrust vs Langsmith — different tradeoffs"
- **Progress updates:** "Week 3: built the eval runner, here's what we learned"
